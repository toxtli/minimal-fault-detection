INTRODUCTION

The concepts of fault modeling, detection, testing, and tolerance have become very important for logic designers. The drastic increase of components in a single chip and as a result of increase in the chip density the probability of fault ocurring also increased.

In this work we are presenting a system based in the stuck-at-faults model that assumes that a fault in a logic gate results in one of its inputs or the output is fixed at either a logic 0 (s-a-0) or at logic 1 (s-a-1).

The stuck-at model was implemented in this work to represent multiple faults in circuits. In a multiple stuck-at-fault, it is assumed that more than one signal line in this circuit are stuck at logic 1 or logic 0. In other words, a group of stuck-at faults exist in the circuit at the same time.
